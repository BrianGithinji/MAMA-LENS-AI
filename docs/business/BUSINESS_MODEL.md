# MAMA-LENS AI — Business Model & Revenue Strategy

## Executive Summary

MAMA-LENS AI is an AI-powered maternal healthcare platform targeting the 1.2 billion people in Africa, where maternal mortality rates are 20x higher than in high-income countries. We combine compassionate AI, telemedicine, and multi-channel accessibility to save lives and reduce maternal mortality.

| Metric | Value |

## Revenue Streams

### 1. B2B — Healthcare Facility Subscriptions
**Target:** Clinics, hospitals, maternity homes, NGO health programs

| Tier | Price/Month | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Basic | $50 | Patient management, risk assessment, appointment booking |
| Professional | $150 | + Telemedicine, wearable integration, analytics |
| Enterprise | $500+ | + Custom AI models, API access, white-labeling |

**Projected Year 1:** 200 facilities × $100 avg = $240,000/year

### 2. B2G — Government Healthcare Partnerships
**Target:** Ministry of Health programs, national maternal health initiatives

- Per-capita licensing for national deployment
- Integration with national health information systems
- Custom analytics dashboards for policy makers
- Training and capacity building

**Projected Year 2:** 3 government contracts × $200,000 = $600,000/year

### 3. NGO & Development Partner Grants
**Target:** UNFPA, WHO, Gates Foundation, USAID, DFID, African Development Bank

- Grant funding for rural deployment
- Impact measurement and reporting
- Research partnerships with universities

**Projected Year 1:** $500,000 in grants

### 4. Telemedicine Revenue Share
**Target:** Individual healthcare providers

- 15% commission on telemedicine consultations
- Average consultation: $5-15 (affordable for African markets)
- Target: 10,000 consultations/month by Year 2

**Projected Year 2:** 10,000 × $10 × 15% = $15,000/month = $180,000/year

### 5. Insurance Integration
**Target:** Health insurance companies, microinsurance providers

- API integration for risk stratification
- Premium calculation based on AI risk scores
- Maternal health insurance product development

### 6. Data Analytics (Anonymized, Consented)
**Target:** Pharmaceutical companies, research institutions, public health agencies

- Aggregated, anonymized maternal health insights
- Disease burden mapping
- Drug efficacy research support

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Kenya (Months 1-12)
- Launch in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu
- Partner with 50 clinics and 5 NGOs
- Community health worker training program
- WhatsApp and USSD deployment for rural areas

### Phase 2: East Africa (Months 12-24)
- Expand to Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda
- Government partnership negotiations
- Swahili language optimization
- Wearable device partnerships

### Phase 3: West & Central Africa (Months 24-48)
- Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, DRC
- French language deployment
- Arabic language for North Africa
- Continental health data network

## Competitive Advantages

1. **Africa-first design** — built for low-connectivity, multilingual, low-literacy contexts
2. **Multi-channel access** — WhatsApp, SMS, USSD, app, web (no smartphone required)
3. **Emotional intelligence** — grief support and mental health integration
4. **Explainable AI** — transparent risk scores that clinicians trust
5. **Community health worker integration** — extends reach to last mile
6. **Open-source core** — builds trust with governments and NGOs
