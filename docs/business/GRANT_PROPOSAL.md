## Problem Statement

Sub-Saharan Africa accounts for 66% of global maternal deaths despite having only 14% of the world's population. The primary causes are preventable:

- **Late detection** of complications (preeclampsia, anemia, gestational diabetes)
- **Limited access** to skilled healthcare providers
- **Geographic barriers** — 60% of African women live more than 5km from a health facility
- **Information gaps** — women lack knowledge of danger signs
- **Emotional trauma** — miscarriage and pregnancy loss receive no structured support
- **Healthcare worker shortages** — 1 doctor per 10,000 people in many regions

### The Three Delays Model
Maternal deaths are caused by three delays:
1. Delay in recognizing danger signs and deciding to seek care
2. Delay in reaching a health facility
3. Delay in receiving adequate care

MAMA-LENS AI addresses all three delays.

## Solution

MAMA-LENS AI is a comprehensive maternal health ecosystem that:

### 1. Early Risk Detection
- AI-powered risk assessment for preeclampsia, anemia, gestational diabetes, miscarriage
- Wearable device integration for continuous monitoring
- Emergency alert system for immediate escalation

### 2. Multi-Channel Accessibility
- Works on any phone via WhatsApp, SMS, and USSD
- No smartphone or internet required for basic features
- Offline-capable mobile app for community health workers

### 3. Emotional Support
- Grief counseling after miscarriage and pregnancy loss
- Mental health screening (Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale)
- 24/7 AI companion for emotional support

### 4. Healthcare Navigation
- GIS-based facility finder
- Emergency routing to nearest maternity facility
- Telemedicine consultations with qualified providers

### 5. Education & Behavior Change
- Week-by-week pregnancy education
- Danger sign awareness in local languages
- Nutrition guidance using locally available foods

## Theory of Change

**If** pregnant women have access to AI-powered risk assessment, multilingual health education, and emergency navigation tools,

**Then** they will recognize danger signs earlier, seek care sooner, and receive appropriate treatment,

**Leading to** reduced maternal and neonatal mortality, improved mental health outcomes, and stronger health systems.
